Scalable Probabilistic Estimates of EV Charging (SPEECh)

Choose from the prepared scenarios or customize your own to generate an EV load profile.

Required Inputs

Number of Electric Vehicles. For reference, the state of California targets 5 million or 8 million by 2030. Today there are about 500,000 electric vehicles out of 30 million light-duty vehicles in the state.

Weekday or Weekend. Represent weekday (Mon-Fri) or weekend (Sat-Sun) charging behaviour in the load profile.

Timers. Remove or leave in the timers in the residential charging segment. Timers delay sessions to start at 11pm or 12am on weekdays to align with the local lowest electricity price period for EV drivers. In the 2019 data, 27% of residential sessions use timers.

Customization

For more information about how these changes and inputs are reflected in the model, please refer to the About page and publication.

For Part 1, ensure the "Custom" scenario is selected. After making adjustments below, click "Submit" above to run your scenario. Note: the values are all reset by refreshing the page or after a run.

Part 1: Adjusting the distribution over driver groups

These driver groups represent distinct behaviors identified in the charging data. Key drivers of the clustering were segment (e.g. frequency of using workplace vs home charging) and battery/energy use.

Example: Adjust the weights of clusters 4, 5, and 6 to sum to 0.5 to generate a scenario where 50% of drivers have access to residential L2 charging.

Note: The distribution should sum to 1. You can change a subset of the values and we will adjust the others for you, keeping the same proportions among the ones you do not edit.

Part 2: Adjusting the distribution over sessions behaviors

The charging behaviors within each driver group and segment are modeled by a mixture model. Click on one to open its model, then adjust the weights in the distribution to alter the mix of behaviors.

Note: The distribution should sum to 1. You can change a subset of the values and we will adjust the others for you, keeping the same proportions among the ones you do not edit.

Note 2: It is possible to change these distributions within a preset scenario, e.g. the BaseCase distribution over driver groups.
